Book recommendations

A few years back, I discovered reading. I was not a prolific reader during my younger years, so I have some catching up to do. However, I mostly read non-fiction. Books that relate to my work, interesests outside work - sports, health, biographies - or parenting - a recent favorite. Bellow is the list of books that helped me be a better profesional. If you have a suggestion that should definetely be on this list, please contact me!

Data Science / Machine Learning

  1. Agile Data Science 2.0 by Russell Jurney
  2. Machine Learning Design Patterns by Valliappa Lakshmanan, Sara Robinson, and Michael Munn
  3. Deep Learning by Ian Goodfellow, Yoshua Bengio, and Aaron Courville
  4. Building Machine Learning Powered Applications by Emmanuel Ameisen

Programming

  1. Effective Python: 90 Specific Ways to Write Better Python by Brett Slatkin
  2. The Pragmatic Programmer by Andrew Hunt and David Thomas
  3. Clean code by Robert C. Martin
  4. The Effective Engineer by Edmond Lau

Product Management

  1. The Lean Product Playbook by Dan Olsen
  2. Lean B2B: Build Products Businesses Want by Etienne Garbugli

Leadership

  1. High Output Management by Andrew S. Grove
  2. Radical Candor by Kim Scott
  3. Extreme Ownership: How U.S. Navy SEALs Lead and Win by Jocko Willink and Leif Babin

Startups / Tech

  1. The Lean Startup by Eric Ries
  2. Chaos Monkeys: Obscene Fortune and Random Failure in Silicon Valley by Antonio García Martínez
  3. How the Internet Happened: From Netscape to the iPhone by Brian McCullough
  4. Bad Blood: Secrets and Lies in a Silicon Valley Startup by John Carreyrou
